AUTONOMOUS ROBOT

I partook in a team project to build a completely autonomous VEX companion robot with the capability of a domestic task: Turning off a stove and bringing a plate to another room. As the mechanical lead, I was in charge of building the robot chassis frame with a functioning single-axis arm in the span of six weeks, taking into account a weight limit.

Mechanical parts consisted mainly of aluminum beams, which are lightweight yet strong in order to minimize final weight. Additionally, I assisted in C-programming of the robotic behaviors, testing and troubleshooting, and writing a final report. Our final robot was able to complete the task in 19 seconds which sets a university record.

SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

I had the unique opportunity of conducting a sensitivity analysis for a senior design team who were building an aircraft for an annual AIAA Design/Build/Fly competition. While not being a direct competition member, I was responsible for helping the seniors determine best aircraft design choices and competition strategies.

The sensitivity study was conducted utilizing MATLAB and noting competition equations that impacted the score. I used a parameter sweep technique to see how changing certain design variables impacted the score and overall aircraft performance. I helped the team determine a 25 lb. total aircraft weight, a 15lb. total payload weight, a 70-85 Watt-hour battery performance, and additional strategies.

ROCKETRY ANALYSIS

I have always been interested in rocketry and 3D-modeling. I created a personal project comparing a high-power rocket from a traditional kit to a fully 3D-printed one with PETG using SOLIDWORKS.

Due to how the rocket connects to the parachute once it deploys, the fin opposite to the eye-bolt hole of the front-centering ring will always strike the ground first at landing. I conducted FEA on this fin to see how it reacts with PETG material.

Additionally, to compare cost, I included all significant middle-end materials and recorded a percentage difference of 11.6% in favor of the 3D-printed fabrication.

RADIAL ENGINE

In my computer aided design course, I designed a radial engine that you would typically see from an aircraft during the piston-engine era using Autodesk Inventor.

Material consisted of steel AISI 4340 242 HR for bearings and plugs. Significant design challenges include intricate details in the piston head and master rod parts.

EPIDEMIC SIMULATION MODEL

During the summer I decided to initiate a software project by creating two versions of a pandemic simulation minigame using C++ and Python.

The game requires strategy and timing in order to win. The goal of the game is to spread your plague to the entire country before doctors find a cure for it!

The format of the program utilizes data structures to track parameter values and relationships. These values change over time through a probability-based logic system and point system where the player can purchase upgrades for their plague and slow down humanity's response. The Python version consists of different abilities for your plague that can help you advance further into the game.

Project challenges include properly syncing plague stats with the appropriate number based on population.
